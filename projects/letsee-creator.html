01

Context & Challenge

"How can we help non-experts create WebAR content without any 3D or coding skills?"

Enterprises were struggling with XR adoption because of technical complexity. B2B content managers and corporate trainers reported relying on outdated text-based manuals that failed to capture detail. Sales teams reportedly spent only 40% of work hours on actual selling : the rest was lost to navigating training material.

02

Research & Insights

Methods

  • Three rounds of wireframe iteration.
  • Benchmarking of similar tools — ZapWorks, 8thWall, Adobe Aero.
  • Internal stakeholder interviews and field feedback.
  • MVP testing with guided activities.

Key insight

Target users wanted "a familiar interface like web content editors," prioritized instant WebAR publishing, and preferred guided templates over freeform 3D manipulation. They didn't want power; they wanted speed.

03

Key Design Decisions

  • Template-first layout : structured templates reduced friction for users without AR or 3D experience.
  • Live preview-first flow : real-time feedback boosted user confidence and shortened the test loop.
  • Lightweight authoring : AR scenes built with minimal objects to keep the canvas legible.
  • No-login onboarding : eliminated the early friction point that killed first-session retention.
Decision
What I changed:
Built the tool around four constraints instead of a freeform 3D editor: a template-first layout, a live preview-first flow, lightweight authoring with minimal objects, and no-login onboarding.
Why
What research or constraint led to it:
Target users(i.e. content managers and trainers) had no AR or 3D experience and wanted a familiar, fast editor. Benchmarking showed freeform 3D tools created the friction that stopped non-experts from publishing, users needed real-time feedback to feel confident, and the login wall was killing first-session retention.
Trade-off
What we gave up:
Power users lose fine-grained 3D control, and the lightweight canvas caps how complex a scene can get outside the template system.
Result
What improved:
Templates and live preview cut friction and boosted confidence, the lighter canvas stayed legible, and removing login eliminated the early drop-off, producing AR content with far less time and resources.

07

Reflection

Letsee Creator was built to lower the barrier to AR content creation and make training more engaging and interactive. While the project involved many technical challenges, the main focus of the project was to leverage an emerging technology to address industries' everyday pain point, contributing to better learning[training] experiences, reducing training costs, and supporting better knowledge retention and performance.

Providing an easy to use experience of an emerging technology was much more challenging than showcasing a novel experience of the technology. Designing with accessibility and simplicity in mind was key to making the tool practical and usable for real-world applications.
